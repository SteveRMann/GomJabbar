Spitting alien, Gom Jabbar Water-watcher from Dune

Spitting snake
Name:  Gom Jabbar Water-Watcher
Home Planet: Arrakis
Species: Ophidia Aquiferra
Classification: Hydrojettic Serpentiform
Temperament: Shy, Gentle, Easily Startled

Overview: Gom Jabbar Water Watchers are slender, serpentine creatures native to the dune grass basins of the Arakeen Verge. Their elongated snouts contain a specialized hydro jet organ capable of releasing narrow streams of water. These jets are harmless and serve as a startle response or mild distress signal. Despite their dramatic height, Water Watchers are timid by nature and prefer to remain partially coiled at ground level, rising only when curious or alarmed.

Locomotion & Behavior: Water Watchers move by uncoiling in smooth, wave like motions, though they typically anchor themselves in a tight base coil for stability. Sudden noises or sharp sounds may startle them, prompting a brief warning trill followed by a reflexive water spurt. Just before releasing the jet, their eyes glow a vivid red—an instinctive visual alert that gives attentive observers a moment to step back. Their vision is excellent, though they avoid direct eye contact, tilting their heads in slow arcs to observe visitors.

Cultural Notes: Local settlers regard Water Watchers as gentle omens of renewal. Ancient desert folklore claims they once guided travelers to hidden springs by spraying in the direction of underground moisture. While unverified, their sensitivity to humidity gradients is remarkable, and they often react moments before a shift in weather.

Zookeeper Advisory: Harmless and shy. Loud noises, sudden movements, or sharp claps may trigger the Water Watcher’s reflexive startle response. If its eyes begin glowing red or you hear a warning trill, the creature is preparing a harmless water “sneeze.” The liquid is clean and non toxic. Approach calmly, speak softly, and avoid leaning directly over the creature’s line of sight.

Warning: If its eyes turn red, the Water Watcher might sneeze. Step back so you don’t get sprinkled!
